Pão de Queijo (Brazilian cheese bread)

Description

Pão de queijo is a traditional Brazilian snack made with tapioca flour and cheese. Crispy on the outside and soft and chewy on the inside, it is naturally gluten-free and commonly enjoyed for breakfast or as an afternoon snack in Brazil.

Ingredients

  • 250 g (2 cups) tapioca flour (polvilho azedo or polvilho doce)
  • 240 ml (1 cup) milk
  • 60 ml (1/4 cup) vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 200 g (2 cups) grated cheese (minas cheese, parmesan, or a mix)

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk, oil, and salt until just boiling.
  2. Place the tapioca flour in a bowl. Pour the hot liquid over it and mix well until combined. Let it cool slightly.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing until smoth. Stir in the grated cheese until you get a sticky dough.
  4. With lightly oiled hands, shape small balls and place them on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 180 °C / 350 °F for 20-25 minutes, until puffed and lightly golden

Serving suggestions

  • Warm and fresh
  • With coffee
  • As breakfast or quick snack
