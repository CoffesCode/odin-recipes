Coxinha (Brazilian cicken croquette)

Coxinha is one of Brazil's most beloved street foods. It consists of a crispy, golden crust filled with seasoned shredded chicken and cream cheese. Crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside, coxinhas are commonly serbed as snacks or party food all over Brazil.

Ingredients

Cicken Filling

  • 500 g(1lb) chicken breast
  • 1 small onion, finelu chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp cream cheese (requeijão)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley

Dough

  • 500 ml (2 cups) chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 300 g (about 2½ cups) all-purpose flour
  • Salt to taste

Breading & Frying

  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions

Prepare the chicken filling

  1. Cook the chicken breast in water with salt until tender
  2. Shred the chicken and set aside
  3. In a pan, heat the olive oil and sauté the onion until translucent
  4. Add the garlic and cook for about 1 minute.
  5. Add the shredded chicken, season with salt and pepper, and mix well
  6. turn off the heat and stir in the cream cheese. Set aside.

Make the dough

  1. In a pot, heat the chicken broth with butter and salt.
  2. When it starts boiling, add the flour all at once
  3. Stir vigorously until the dough pulls away from the pan and forms a smoth ball.
  4. Transfer to a surface and let it cool slightly

Shape the coxinhas

  1. Take a small portion of dough and flatten it in your hand.
  2. Add a spoonful of filling in the center
  3. Close and shape it into a teardrop form (classic coxinha shape)

Bread and fry

  1. Dip each coxinha in beaten eggs
  2. Coat with breadcrumbs
  3. Fry in hot oil (180 °C / 350 °F) until golden brown.
  4. Drain on paper towels

Serving suggestions

  • As party snacks
  • With hot sauce
  • Or alongside other Brazilian finger foods
